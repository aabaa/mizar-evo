# Architecture: VC Generation

> Canonical language: English. Japanese companion: [../ja/07.vc_generation.md](../ja/07.vc_generation.md).

## Purpose

This document defines how Mizar Evo prepares algorithm bodies for verification, turns core proof goals and correctness conditions into `VcIr`, and discharges deterministic obligations before ATP translation.

It refines phases 10, 11, and 12 of [00.pipeline_overview.md](./00.pipeline_overview.md). In particular, it defines the boundary between backend-neutral `CoreIr`, algorithm-specific `ControlFlowIr`, prover-independent `VcIr`, and the pre-ATP discharge result that selects what ATP translation receives.

## Design Decisions

### VC Generation Is Prover-Independent

`VcIr` is the last Mizar-side representation before ATP translation. It records goals, premises, local context, source maps, and proof hints without committing to TPTP, SMT-LIB, or a concrete backend profile.

Phase 11 VC generation owns:

- converting `ObligationSeedId`s into concrete `VcId`s;
- preparing algorithm control-flow facts;
- generating proof obligations from proof skeleton leaves;
- generating correctness obligations for definitions, redefinitions, registrations, and reductions;
- generating algorithm contract, invariant, assertion, call precondition, and termination obligations;
- attaching local premise sets and cited references.

Phase 12 pre-ATP discharge owns:

- attempting deterministic discharge for canonical `VcIr`;
- assigning pre-ATP `VcStatus` values;
- preserving ATP-bound VCs without weakening their contexts.

It does not own:

- name, type, registration, overload, or elaboration decisions;
- ATP encoding or backend selection;
- external process management;
- certificate validation;
- stable artifact emission.

### Obligation Seeds Become VCs Exactly Once

Phase 9 creates obligation seeds but not `VcId`s. Phase 11 is the single owner of `VcId` assignment.

Each seed is converted into zero or more VCs:

- zero when the seed is policy-disabled or belongs to an invalid skipped item;
- one for ordinary theorem/proof/definition goals;
- multiple when a single source construct expands into several checks, such as loop invariant establishment and maintenance.

The seed-to-VC mapping is recorded so diagnostics, LSP metadata, artifacts, and incremental builds can trace every VC back to source.

### ControlFlowIr Is Algorithm-Specific

Algorithm bodies need information that theorem proofs do not: mutation, branching, loops, early exits, ghost state, and termination measures. Phase 10 builds `ControlFlowIr` before VC generation.

`ControlFlowIr` owns:

- basic blocks and edges;
- local variable assignment state;
- return, break, and continue exits;
- loop headers and cutpoints;
- invariant and decreasing annotations attached to loop structure;
- ghost/runtime use classification;
- call sites and their contract instantiations.

It does not own proof results. It only prepares the structured program facts needed by phase 11.

### VCs Preserve Local Context Explicitly

Every VC has a self-contained local context:

- introduced binders and type predicates;
- assumptions from proof skeletons;
- algorithm path conditions;
- available loop invariants;
- cited premises from `by` justifications;
- facts derived from type checking and registration traces;
- definition expansion policy and selected unfoldings.

The context is symbolic and canonical. ATP translation may later choose how to encode or prune premises, but it must not reconstruct semantic context from source syntax.

### Pre-ATP Discharge Is Deterministic and Explainable

Phase 12 handles obligations that Mizar-side machinery can solve without external search.

Allowed discharge sources include:

- syntactic tautologies and contradiction checks;
- reflexivity and direct equality normalization;
- type facts already present in `TypeFactTable`;
- cluster facts with replayable `ResolutionTrace`;
- definitional reductions permitted by expansion policy;
- bounded computation for `by computation` under configured gas and timeout.

Pre-ATP discharge may also assign policy statuses such as `AssumedByPolicy` or `PolicyOpen`, but these are not discharge evidence. Discharge must record an explanation. Phase 12 must not silently drop VCs. VCs that require external search are marked `NeedsAtp` and continue to ATP translation with their source, local context, and proof hints intact.

### Definition Unfolding Is Controlled

VC generation may unfold definitions only through explicit expansion policy from `CoreIr`.

The policy controls:

- whether the definition is opaque or transparent by default;
- whether a reduction registration allows simplification;
- whether a computable algorithm may be evaluated in `by computation`;
- whether a proof hint requests local unfolding for a specific VC.

This prevents accidental changes in proof strength or ATP problem size caused by traversal order.

### Algorithm VCs Follow Structured Control Flow

Algorithm verification uses a Hoare-style schema over `ControlFlowIr`.

The generator creates VCs for:

- `requires` at call sites;
- `ensures` at each return;
- `assert` at the program point where it appears;
- invariant establishment before entering a loop;
- invariant maintenance for each loop back edge;
- invariant availability on normal loop exit;
- invariant preservation at `break` and `continue`;
- decreasing measure non-negativity and strict decrease;
- finiteness and order-independence requirements for collection loops;
- ghost erasure safety.

For recursive and mutually recursive algorithms, termination obligations are grouped by call graph component and decreasing measure.

### Status and Policy Are Reflected in VCs

Theorem statuses affect VC generation and discharge.

- `assumed` items produce artifact metadata and premises according to policy, but no proof VC for their proposition.
- `open` items may produce open proof status without ATP dispatch.
- `conditional` items must record their non-clean dependencies.
- ordinary theorems must not depend on non-clean items.

Policy decisions must be represented explicitly in `VcStatus`, not hidden as missing obligations.

## VC Pipeline

### Step 1: Build ControlFlowIr

Input:

- core algorithm shells from `CoreIr`;
- lowered contracts and statement trees;
- ghost/runtime metadata;
- source maps.

Output:

- `ControlFlowIr` per algorithm;
- algorithm diagnostics;
- statement-to-block source maps.

Responsibilities:

- construct basic blocks and control-flow edges;
- resolve normal, return, break, and continue exits;
- attach loop invariants and decreasing measures to loop headers;
- track assignment state and use-before-assignment;
- classify ghost-only expressions and illegal ghost-to-runtime flows;
- preserve statement order for reproducible diagnostics.

### Step 2: Collect Obligation Seeds

Input:

- `CoreIr`;
- `ControlFlowIr`;
- obligation seed table;
- theorem and registration status metadata.

Output:

- ordered `ObligationSeed`s ready for VC generation;
- seed dependency graph.

Responsibilities:

- collect proof skeleton terminal goals;
- collect definition and registration correctness seeds;
- collect algorithm contract and termination seeds implied by CFG structure;
- remove seeds belonging to skipped invalid items;
- sort seeds deterministically by module, item, source range, and seed kind.

### Step 3: Generate Theorem and Definition VCs

Input:

- proof trees and theorem propositions;
- definition and registration core items;
- local proof contexts;
- cited labels and premises.

Output:

- `VcIr` for theorem, proof-step, definition, and registration obligations.

Responsibilities:

- instantiate theorem binders and type predicates;
- convert terminal proof goals into VC goals;
- attach cited premises from `by` justifications;
- generate existence, uniqueness, coherence, compatibility, consistency, and reducibility obligations;
- record status dependencies for clean/non-clean checks.

### Step 4: Generate Algorithm VCs

Input:

- `ControlFlowIr`;
- algorithm contracts;
- call graph and termination metadata.

Output:

- `VcIr` for algorithm obligations;
- algorithm-specific diagnostic metadata.

Responsibilities:

- generate precondition VCs for calls;
- generate postcondition VCs for returns;
- generate assertion VCs;
- generate loop invariant establishment and maintenance VCs;
- generate break/continue invariant VCs;
- generate decreasing measure and termination VCs;
- generate ghost erasure safety VCs;
- encode path conditions as local context, not as backend-specific formulas.

### Step 5: Normalize and Classify VCs

Input:

- raw VCs;
- local contexts;
- source maps;
- proof hints.

Output:

- canonical `VcIr`;
- `VcIndex`;
- reproducible VC ordering.

Responsibilities:

- normalize alpha-equivalent binders;
- sort premises by stable references;
- attach source ranges and obligation seed provenance;
- classify VC kind and priority;
- attach proof hints and computation limits;
- assign deterministic `VcId`s.

### Step 6: Pre-ATP Discharge

Input:

- canonical `VcIr`;
- local type and cluster facts;
- definition expansion policy;
- computation engine configuration.

Output:

- discharged VCs with explanations;
- `NeedsAtp` VCs for ATP translation;
- deterministic discharge diagnostics.

Responsibilities:

- solve trivial and type-derived goals;
- replay cluster-derived facts using `ResolutionTrace`;
- apply allowed reductions and definitional simplifications;
- evaluate `by computation` goals within configured limits;
- mark policy-controlled assumed/open statuses;
- preserve ATP-bound VCs without weakening their context.

## Interface Definitions

### ControlFlowIr

```rust
struct ControlFlowIr {
    algorithm: SymbolId,
    blocks: Vec<BasicBlock>,
    locals: LocalTable,
    contracts: ContractSet,
    loops: Vec<LoopInfo>,
    exits: Vec<ExitInfo>,
    ghost_effects: GhostEffectTable,
    termination: Option<TerminationPlan>,
    source_map: ControlFlowSourceMap,
}
```

### Basic Block

```rust
struct BasicBlock {
    id: BasicBlockId,
    statements: Vec<CoreStatementId>,
    terminator: Terminator,
    context_in: ProgramContextId,
    context_out: Vec<ProgramContextId>,
}

enum Terminator {
    Goto(BasicBlockId),
    Branch { condition: CoreFormulaId, then_bb: BasicBlockId, else_bb: BasicBlockId },
    Return(Option<CoreTermId>),
    Break(LoopId),
    Continue(LoopId),
    Unreachable,
}
```

### Obligation Seed

```rust
struct ObligationSeed {
    id: ObligationSeedId,
    origin: ObligationOrigin,
    source: CoreSourceRef,
    local_context: CoreContextSnapshot,
    goal: CoreFormulaId,
    hints: Vec<ProofHint>,
}

enum ObligationOrigin {
    ProofStep { theorem: SymbolId, proof_node: CoreProofNodeId },
    DefinitionCorrectness { symbol: SymbolId, condition: CorrectnessKind },
    RegistrationCorrectness { registration: RegistrationId, condition: CorrectnessKind },
    AlgorithmContract { algorithm: SymbolId, site: AlgorithmSiteId, kind: AlgorithmVcKind },
    Termination { algorithm: SymbolId, call_or_loop: TerminationSiteId },
}
```

### VC IR

```rust
struct VcIr {
    id: VcId,
    seed: ObligationSeedId,
    source_range: SourceRange,
    kind: VcKind,
    local_context: LocalContext,
    premises: Vec<PremiseRef>,
    goal: CoreFormulaId,
    proof_hint: Option<ProofHint>,
}

enum VcKind {
    TheoremStep,
    DefinitionCorrectness(CorrectnessKind),
    RegistrationCorrectness(CorrectnessKind),
    AlgorithmContract(AlgorithmVcKind),
    Termination,
    TypeOrSethood,
}
```

### VC Status

```rust
enum VcStatus {
    PolicyOpen(PolicyRef),
    Discharged(DischargeEvidence),
    NeedsAtp,
    SkippedDueToInvalidInput,
    AssumedByPolicy(PolicyRef),
}

struct DischargeEvidence {
    method: DischargeMethod,
    premises: Vec<PremiseRef>,
    trace: Option<ResolutionTraceRef>,
}
```

`NeedsAtp` is the phase 13 input state. `PolicyOpen` records an intentionally open obligation that is not sent to ATP under the active policy. `AssumedByPolicy` records an accepted assumption, not proof evidence. `VcStatus` is internal until artifact emission. Kernel-accepted proof status is assigned later.

## Diagnostics

VC diagnostics are generated before ATP translation.

| Diagnostic Class | Example |
|---|---|
| CFG construction | unreachable statement, use before assignment, illegal break |
| ghost/runtime separation | ghost value flows into runtime expression |
| obligation generation | missing loop invariant, unsupported statement form |
| termination | decreasing measure missing, not Nat-valued, not decreasing |
| context construction | cited premise unavailable, local fact escapes scope |
| pre-ATP discharge | computation limit exceeded, recursive definitional expansion |
| policy | open or assumed item used where clean proof is required |

Diagnostics should include:

- source span of the obligation seed;
- generated `VcId` when available;
- local context summary for debug views;
- related loop, call, theorem, or registration source spans;
- deterministic discharge explanation or failure reason.

## Incrementality and Reproducibility

VC cache keys include:

- `CoreIr` hash;
- `ControlFlowIr` hash for algorithm obligations;
- obligation seed hashes;
- relevant type fact and resolution trace hashes;
- verifier policy and computation limit settings;
- VC schema version.

Changes invalidate:

- VCs generated from changed obligation seeds;
- algorithm VCs whose CFG, contracts, or termination metadata changed;
- downstream ATP problems for changed `NeedsAtp` VCs;
- artifacts that record changed VC status or diagnostics.

Ordering must be deterministic:

- obligation seeds are sorted by module, item, source range, and origin kind;
- generated `VcId`s are assigned from the sorted seed list and per-seed expansion index;
- premises are sorted by canonical `PremiseRef`;
- discharged, policy-status, and `NeedsAtp` VC lists preserve VC order;
- diagnostics with the same source range are ordered by VC kind and diagnostic code.

## Alternatives Considered

### Generate VCs Directly from ResolvedTypedAst

Generating VCs from source-shaped syntax would preserve surface details but would duplicate elaboration logic and make proof contexts depend on notation. The adopted design generates VCs from `CoreIr` and source maps.

### Combine ControlFlowIr and CoreIr

Putting CFG data directly into `CoreIr` would make algorithm verification simpler to call, but it would force non-algorithm consumers to handle program-analysis details. The adopted design keeps algorithm-specific flow in `ControlFlowIr`.

### Send All VCs to ATP

Delegating every obligation to ATP would simplify phase 12, but it would be slower, less deterministic, and would hide type/cluster explanations. The adopted design discharges deterministic obligations before ATP translation.

### Do Pre-ATP Discharge During VC Generation

Interleaving generation and discharge can save memory, but it makes diagnostics and scheduling harder to reproduce. The adopted design produces canonical `VcIr` first, then performs deterministic discharge as a separate step.

## Adopted Approach

Mizar Evo uses a three-stage verification preparation boundary.

Phase 10 constructs `ControlFlowIr` for algorithm bodies. Phase 11 converts obligation seeds from `CoreIr` and `ControlFlowIr` into canonical `VcIr` with deterministic `VcId`s. Phase 12 discharges obligations that can be solved by deterministic Mizar-side machinery, assigns policy statuses where configured, and forwards only `NeedsAtp` VCs to ATP translation.

## Constraints and Assumptions

- VC generation starts only after elaboration has produced `CoreIr`.
- Phase 10 may reject invalid algorithm control-flow before VCs are generated.
- Phase 11 is the only phase that assigns `VcId`s.
- Phase 12 may discharge VCs or assign policy statuses, but must not erase ATP-bound VCs or weaken their contexts.
- ATP translation receives only canonical `VcIr` with `NeedsAtp` status.
- Pre-ATP discharge must be deterministic for identical source, dependencies, toolchain, policy, and computation limits.
